Welcome to Mindkind

Mental health is the state of mental well-being that helps people cope with the everyday stresses of life, realise their full potention, learn new things, work well and contribute to society.

At Mindkind, we believe that mental health deserves compassion, understanding, and everyday visibility. We’re a community-rooted initiative built around the idea that kindness—toward ourselves and one another—can shape how we experience healing. Whether you're navigating anxiety, grief, depression, PTSD or other mental health issues, or are simply seeking space to breathe, Mindkind offers resources, and support designed to be inclusive and calming. We’re here to help everyone feel seen, heard, and valued.

Common Issues

1 in 4 people will experience a mental health problem of some kind each year in England. Here are a few examples of issues facing many people today.

Anxiety

Common symptoms of anxiety include:

  • A feeling of panic or danger
  • Having trouble sleeping
  • Unable to stay calm and still
  • Cold, sweaty or tingling hands or feet
  • Having trouble breathing (both shortness of breath and breathing faster than normal)
  • Increased heart rate
  • Dizziness or feeling weak

Depression

Common symptoms of depression include:

  • Continuous low mood or sadness
  • Feeling hopeless and helpless
  • Having low self-esteem
  • Feeling tearful, guilt-ridden, irritable and intolerant of others
  • Saving no motivation or interest in things
  • Finding it difficult to make decisions

Grief

Common symptoms of grief include:

  • Fatigue
  • Changes in appetite
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Feelings of loneliness and anger
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Memory problems

PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder)

Common symptoms of PTSD include:

  • Flashbacks
  • Nightmares
  • Physical sensations, such as pain, sweating, feeling sick or trembling
  • Feeling tense, on guard, or on edge
  • Having difficulty concentrating
  • Having difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
  • Feeling irritable and having angry or aggressive outbursts

Wellbeing Tips

Here are some tips for how you can improve your mental health and reduce stress.

Get Better Sleep

  • Establish a consistent sleep schedule
  • Create a relaxing bedtime routine
  • Optimize your sleep environment
  • Avoid substances that interfere with sleep

Meditation

  • Find a quiet, comfortable space
  • Set aside a short amount of time
  • Focus on your breath
  • Don't get discouraged by distractions

Exercise

  • Any type of exercise is useful
  • Make small changes to your daily routine
  • Incorporate more movement into everyday activities
  • Find an exercise you enjoy

Eat well

  • Eat regularly
  • Stay hydrated
  • Eat the right balance of fats
  • Include more whole grains, fruits and vegetables in your diet
  • Be aware of how caffeine can affect your mood

Nature

  • Get out into nature
  • Find nature wherever you are
  • Use all of your senses
  • Exercise in nature
  • Protect nature

Connect

  • Spend meaningful time with loved ones
  • Be present and actively listen
  • Join clubs or groups
  • Attend local events
  • Volunteer
